How to Play

Applied multiplayer decision making, from role assignment to simulator-assisted learning.

Threat assessment

Threat assessment is not the same as “who looks scary.” It is the process of identifying who benefits most from waiting, who is most likely to convert first, and who is currently changing the table’s options. Correct threat assessment is what lets you decide where pressure, interaction, or patience actually belong.

Role assignment

Your role is not fixed. In one pod you may be the beats deck. In another you may be the player trying to survive long enough to change the status quo. Polyhedron places heavy emphasis on role assignment because many losses come from playing the wrong game, not from drawing the wrong cards.

When to go for it

Combo goes for it when timing is right. Aggro goes for it in a broader sense by expanding pressure and forcing responses. Control often wants somebody else to go first so that interaction can be applied at the highest-leverage moment. Midrange goes for it opportunistically once the table has clarified who is actually under pressure.

The live game simulator

The live game simulator is more than a life counter. It is a learning tool built around the same value set as the framework:

  • Life counter — establishes a clear baseline for the pod.
  • Threat assessment tool — helps signal who is currently ahead or applying the most structural pressure.
  • Judge feature — helps reduce rules friction so players can keep games moving and keep learning.
Why this matters

Good tools accelerate good habits. When players can more clearly see life totals, commander damage, poison, and who must act, they learn faster. When players can resolve rules questions without derailing the whole pod, they maintain momentum and spend more time actually playing.
