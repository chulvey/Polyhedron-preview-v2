Archetypes

The exploit poles and the center posture, explained in Polyhedron language without reducing them to internet shorthand.

Combo (Assembly)

Combo is A + B + C. The pieces are specific, different, and additive. The deck is trying to assemble distinct functions that together produce a decisive result. Combo “goes for it” conditionally: it wants the right timing window, not merely any moment with enough mana.

Strong combo play is about knowing what role each piece serves, what remains missing, and when the table has finally given you permission to commit.

Aggro (Compression)

Aggro is A, A, A, A. Its pieces are often similar, redundant, and functionally overlapping. Aggro can absolutely combine cards, but it does not need one exact assembly path to matter. It “goes for it” more broadly: it uses initiative, threat assessment, and target sequencing to decide where pressure should land first.

Strong aggro play is about identifying who benefits most from waiting, then denying that player time.

Control (Suppression)

Control wins by deriving advantage through superior card quality and application over time. It uses patience, interaction, and selective suppression to keep the table from converting cleanly. Control does not need every card to be explosive. It needs the right answer at the right time, and the discipline not to solve problems that are not yet real.

Strong control play is about knowing what matters, what can wait, and when the table has finally been pushed into your timing rather than its own.

Midrange (Stability)

Midrange sits at the center. It is not a vague blend of everything else; it is a stability posture that trades some peak ceiling for reduced exploitability, flexibility, and role-shifting. Midrange wins by staying live across changing game states and refusing to become the easiest player to punish.

Strong midrange play is about role recognition: pressure when waiting favors someone else, stabilize when contraction is happening too quickly, and preserve flexibility until the best line becomes clear.
